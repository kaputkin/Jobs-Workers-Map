This map uses data from the Longitudinal Employer-Household Dynamics program which is part of the economic census. It reveals the concentration of certain industries versus the dispersion of others. It also reveals the concentration of workers from particular industries in particular parts of the city.

Use the slider to view a before and after effect of 2005 data on the left and 2015 data on the left.

The 10 minute walkshed layer is a simple estimate of the area that is walkable within 10 minutes of a subway station.
The jobs sectors refer to the North American Industry Calssification System (NAICS) code. They are broad categories based on the activity a firm is primarily engaged in. Some categories are broadly defined, like Professional, which includes anyone from lawyers to graphic designers to biology researchers.

For information on what each category specifically refers to, visit the 2017 NAICS codebook to view each category in depth.
